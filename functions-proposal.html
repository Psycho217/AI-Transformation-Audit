Why all 7 functions — and why in this order

A business has exactly 7 functions. The first three form the revenue engine — they come first. The next four protect, sustain, grow, and steer. Skip any one and the business develops a blind spot that compounds.

Survival business vs. growth business

A business is either in survival mode (all energy goes to paying monthly expenses) or growth mode (survival happens by default, energy goes to scale). Ziiroo must build as a growth business from day one.

Survival businessGrowth business (what we build)
Focused only on monthly survival — paying rent, bills, suppliersFocused on scale and expansion — survival is a byproduct
Great at product/service, but poor at marketing & salesBalance: great product/service AND great income-generating skills
Reactive marketing — word-of-mouth only, inconsistent income80% marketing (value addition), 20% selling (promotion)
Owner-driven — one person does everything, bottleneckFunction-driven — systems and teams run each function

Critical rule — the 80/20 of marketing vs. sales: 80% of effort goes to value addition (education, content, building credibility). 20% goes to promotion (pitching, selling). Only sales fails. Without value addition first, even good products get ignored.

Function 6

HR

Recruit, develop, and retain the right people. HR isn't admin — it's the function that determines whether you can scale beyond yourself.

Pain Without structured HR

  • Hiring on gut feel — no assessment framework, wrong hires cost 3-5x their salary in wasted time
  • No onboarding — new people take months to become productive, or leave early
  • No performance tracking — can't tell who's growing and who's coasting
  • Retention problems — good people leave because there's no growth path, development, or recognition
  • Culture by accident — values undefined, behavior standards unclear, toxicity creeps in

Solution HR framework for Ziiroo

The ASK hiring system — hire talent, not cheap labor

1. List every specific task the role requires
2. For each task, define: Attitude + Skill + Knowledge needed
3. Design assessments: Psychometric (A) · Assignment (S) · Test (K)
4. Hire the person who scores highest — pay them what they're worth

One talented person at premium salary > five people at ₹30K who need you to do the work. The wrong hire doesn't just cost their salary — they cost your time, your clients' trust, and your growth momentum. Hire slow, fire fast.

Recruit

  • ASK assessment sheets
  • Structured interview process

Retain

Function 7

Management

Strategy, functional oversight, and people leadership. Management is the function that makes all other functions work together — and keeps them aligned to the vision goal.

Pain Without structured management

  • No strategic planning — reacting to opportunities instead of creating them
  • Firefighting mode — every day is urgent, nothing is important
  • Functions drift — marketing, sales, ops each doing their own thing with no alignment
  • No review cadence — problems compound silently until crisis
  • Owner bottleneck — every decision waits for the founder, nothing moves independently

Solution Management framework for Ziiroo

Strategy Management

Quarterly goals. Vision goal → functional goals → weekly priorities. Review what's working, kill what's not. This is the founder's #1 job.

Functional Management

Build systems in each function, then review them. Two functions at a time. Over 3 years, all 7 become self-running with SOPs, dashboards, and owners.

People Management

Give feedback. Hold people accountable. Fire the wrong people quickly. Reward the right ones generously. One-on-ones weekly, reviews quarterly.

The vision goal — your North Star number

Founder's Role

Your role as founder — management, not firefighting

Doing everything yourself is not commitment — it's a trap. Your job is management: strategy, functions, and people.

The 3 responsibilities of the founder

1. Strategy management

Create strategies for scale, improving profit margins, and improving cash flows. This is your #1 priority — nobody else will do it.

2. Functional management

Build systems and teams in each function. Then review them. Two functions at a time. Over 3 years, all 7 functions become self-running.

3. People management

Give feedback, hold people accountable, fire the wrong people, reward the right ones. Build a hiring system: list tasks → define attitude/skill/knowledge → prepare assessments.

The self-employment trap: You start a business, hire people, pay salaries — but end up doing the work your team should do. You become the bottleneck. Revenue stalls. The cure: management + one function you're best at. Hire for the other two. One person at a time, one department at a time.

3 key changes to build Ziiroo as a growth business

ChangeWhat it means for ZiirooWhen
1. Enhance clarity Define a vision goal (revenue number worth giving years of your life to). Reverse-engineer functional goals — what must marketing, sales, ops each achieve to reach that number. This week
2. Enhance capability Learn to create a profitable, scalable business model. Learn to design systems (SOPs, dashboards, trackers) in every function. Learn to hire, appraise, and fire. Months 1-3
3. Enhance courage Courage to give fairly (pay for talent). Courage to make decisions (don't wait for perfect conditions). Courage to invest the money you don't have to make the money you desire. Always

Invest in 4 areas: Marketing (increase reach), Hiring (delegate to talented people), Automating (become efficient), Learning (make better decisions). These are investments, not expenses. Cutting them paralyzes growth.
